\begin{exercise}

Consider the planes defined by the equations below.

\[
\begin{array}{lr}
P_1:  \quad x-2y+z &=3 \\
P_2:  \quad  2x-y+6z &= 1 \\
P_3:  \quad  3y+6z &=5 \\
\end{array}
\]

The planes $P_1$ and $P_2$ are \wordChoice{\choice{parallel}\choice{orthogonal}\choice[correct]{neither parallel nor orthogonal}}.

The planes $P_1$ and $P_3$ are \wordChoice{\choice{parallel}\choice[correct]{orthogonal}\choice{neither parallel nor orthogonal}}.

The planes $P_2$ and $P_3$ are \wordChoice{\choice{parallel}\choice{orthogonal}\choice[correct]{neither parallel nor orthogonal}}.

\end{exercise}
